## What Larry Does (The Full System)

From the YouTube video "Give OpenClaw this 1 skill" (12:40 min) and X posts, here's the full breakdown:

### Core Workflow
1. **Researches competitors** — Larry scans accounts Oliver follows on X/Twitter for content about slideshows and TikTok virality
2. **Generates content** based on what works — creates TikTok slideshows (app screenshots + AI-generated room photos)
3. **A/B tests hooks** — iterates on opening lines to find what gets views (e.g., "My landlord wouldn't let me decorate until...", "I showed my boyfriend...", "My flatmate moved out and left me the ugliest room...")
4. **Posts as drafts** — posts to TikTok via API as drafts, Oliver selects music/trends manually, then publishes from his phone (so TikTok treats it as organic, not bot)
5. **Tracks analytics** — can pull revenue, new users, and paying subscribers from app analytics
6. **Learns what converts** — not just what gets views, but what leads to app downloads and paying users

### Key Metrics
- **8M views in one week** (viral peak)
- **$4,000 earned in 24 hours** at one point
- **400K views** on single best-performing TikTok
- **MRR jumped to $714** from these efforts

### The Winning Formula (TikTok Slideshows)
- **Hook type that worked:** Landlord/boyfriend/flatmate blocking decoration + AI room reveal
- **Slide structure:** Hook slide → "nothing" slide (forces 50% watch time) → reveal slide → CTA slide
- **CTA that works:** Don't say "link in bio" — tell a story ("Thank god for the Snugly app, it finally helped me convince her")
- **Images:** AI-generated room photos (even with flaws that trigger comment engagement, boosting algorithm)

### Why Post as Drafts?
- Direct API posts = no music = killed reach
- Direct API posts = TikTok flags as spam = reduced reach
- Draft + manual publish = full algorithm boost + music/trend selection

### Warm-Up Requirement
- TikTok account needs 30 min/day warm-up (browsing, liking, commenting as a human) for at least 2 weeks before Larry starts posting
