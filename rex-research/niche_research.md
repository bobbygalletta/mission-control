## 1. What's Oversaturated (Avoid These)

These niches are already packed with creators, courses, and low-quality content:

- **Basic ChatGPT tutorials** — "How to use ChatGPT" is dead. Everyone and their mother has a tutorial. No CPM.
- **Generic "AI Money" content** — The "prompt engineering to get rich" crowd. Oversaturated, skeptical audience, low trust.
- **Faceless YouTube automation channels** (general) — Channels teaching others to do faceless automation are everywhere. The "cash cow" niche itself is now a cash cow *market*. Too many teachers, not enough students who succeed.
- **"Make Money with AI" TikTok accounts** — Every scroll shows someone claiming $10K/month with AI. Audience is burned out. Trust is near zero.
- **Pure AI art/image generation tutorials** — Commoditized. Midjourney prompts for free are everywhere.
- **General vibe coding / no-code app building** — Alex Finn owns this space on YouTube. Big time. Don't compete directly.

## 4. The Gaps / Opportunities

### What's NOT being covered by major AI YouTube creators:

1. **AI for Local Service Businesses** — Completely untapped. Think: dentists, HVAC companies, plumbers, electricians, salons, chiropractors. They all have the same problem: missed calls, no-shows, slow admin. They can't build it themselves. They need someone to show them.

2. **AI Agents for Specific Professions** — Accountants, lawyers, real estate agents, insurance agents. These are small businesses with real budgets and real problems. Content showing "I built a dental office AI booking agent" would get traction AND convert.

3. **"AI Team" Lifestyle Brand** — Bobby has an actual AI team. No one on YouTube/TikTok is doing this credibly with an actual team setup. This is a genuinely unique angle that nobody can easily replicate.

4. **AI Micro-SaaS "Built in Public"** — Find one tiny problem (appointment reminders, lead capture, follow-up texts), build the tool fast, document the whole build, launch it. Content IS the marketing. Very few people are doing this well on TikTok.

5. **AI for Real Estate Agents** — This is NOT the same as "real estate investing." Local real estate agents are solo/small shop operators who desperately need AI for lead follow-up, listing descriptions, CRM updates. Low competition on social, high willingness to pay.

## 5. Concrete Niche Recommendations

---

### NICHE #1: AI Agents for Local Service Businesses
**"I build AI workers for [dentists / HVAC / salons] — here's what they do"**

#### Why It Works
- **Massive underserved market:** 30+ million local service businesses in the US alone. Almost NO content is reaching them in AI language they understand.
- **Real pain points:** Missed calls = lost revenue. Every dentist, HVAC company, and plumber has this problem. They NEED AI voice agents, booking bots, and SMS reminders.
- **High ticket items:** These businesses will pay $500-$2,000/month for AI tools that solve their problems. You're not selling a $9.99 course — you're selling a transformation.
- **Low competition:** Search "AI for dentists YouTube" or "AI for HVAC TikTok." Empty. You're early.
- **Easy to demo:** Show the actual AI answering a call, booking an appointment, confirming a job. Visual + concrete = viral.

#### How to Monetize
1. **YouTube/TikTok** → Build trust with before/after demos, "AI worker of the week" format, tool reviews for this specific audience
2. **Lead gen** → Offer free AI audit → Sell done-for-you setup ($2,000-$10,000)
3. **Templates + guides** → "The AI Dental Office Starter Kit" on Gumroad ($97-$497)
4. **Affiliate** → Phone systems (Twilio, RingCentral), scheduling tools (Calendly alternatives), AI voice platforms
5. **Course** → "AI-Powered Local Service Business" ($297-$997)

#### Who Else Is Doing It
- Almost nobody on YouTube/TikTok specifically targeting local service businesses with AI agent content
- Some agency channels exist but they're generic "automate your business" — no vertical focus

#### Difficulty: Medium
- Need to learn the specific workflows of each industry (or find a partner who knows dental/HVAC)
- Content format is demonstrable: screen recordings of AI agents, call recordings (with permission), dashboard views

### NICHE #2: AI Micro-SaaS Built in Public
**"I found a tiny problem. I built a tool in 3 days. Here's the revenue report."**

#### Why It Works
- **Proven format:** The "build in public" / "indie hacker" niche works. People love watching tools get built. Micro-SaaS is a proven business model.
- **Unique angle for Bobby:** Bobby has an AI TEAM. Most indie hackers are solo. Showcasing an AI-assisted build process is genuinely novel content. "Day 1: Prompted the agent to build our lead qualifier. Day 2: Tested it. Day 3: 12 users." Nobody is doing this with a team.
- **Monetization is built-in:** The SaaS IS the product. Revenue reports make compelling content.
- **Dual income stream:** YouTube ad revenue + the actual product revenue
- **"Solved" niche potential:** Once you build 3-4 micro-SaaS in public, you become the authority on rapid AI app building

#### How to Monetize
1. **The tool itself** — $9-$49/month subscriptions. Solve one tiny problem per tool.
2. **YouTube revenue** — Build videos, revenue updates, problem → solution format
3. **Course/community** — "Build AI Micro-SaaS in 30 Days" ($497-$1,497)
4. **Template packs** — "Prompts and workflows to build your micro-SaaS" ($97-$297)

#### Who Else Is Doing It
- Some indie hackers on Twitter/YouTube (Pieter Levels, etc.) but mostly solo builds
- Nobody doing this with an AI AGENT TEAM as the differentiator
- TikTok micro-SaaS content is nascent — big opportunity

#### Difficulty: Hard
- Need to actually build/ship products
- Requires some technical literacy (but Bobby's AI team helps)
- First few builds might flop (that's fine, it's content)
